# Archetype Quick Reference
## Character Types in The Thirteen Towers

---

## What Are Archetypes?

Archetypes are **ethical and moral frameworks** that define:
- How you approach problems
- What you risk and protect
- Your core values and methods
- When you get Edge bonuses

**Important**: Archetypes are NOT combat classes. Any Archetype can develop any Domain Tree. They define your approach, not your capabilities.

## The Seven Archetypes

### âš”ï¸ Shieldbearer
**The Protector**

**Core Identity**: You stand between danger and those who can't protect themselves.

**You value**:
- Protection of the vulnerable
- Holding the line when others would flee
- Physical courage and sacrifice
- Being the wall others shelter behind

**You get Edge (+1-2) when**:
- Protecting others from harm
- Holding a defensive position
- Interposing yourself between danger and allies
- Taking damage meant for someone else

**Questions for your character**:
- Who or what are you sworn to protect?
- What would make you abandon your post?
- How do you define "innocent"?

**Example concepts**:
- Bodyguard who takes contracts seriously
- Community defender standing against raiders
- Intelligence agent protecting sources
- Parent protecting their children

## Using Your Archetype

### In Play

1. **Describe your approach** using your Archetype's values
2. **Claim Edge** when acting in your archetypal mode
3. **Face challenges** when your values conflict with pragmatism

### Edge from Archetypes

Archetype Edge is usually **+1 to +2** when you're clearly acting in character.

**Example**:
> Shieldbearer jumping in front of an arrow meant for an ally: +2 Edge to Body roll to intercept

> Patternweaver who prepared trap using gathered intelligence: +2 Edge to spring the trap effectively

### Archetypes and Domains

**Any Archetype can pursue any Domain.**

- **Shieldbearer Covert Operative**: Protects intelligence sources and networks
- **Songbearer Economic Factor**: Uses cultural knowledge to broker trade deals
- **Patternweaver Martial Commander**: Analyzes battle patterns and plans strategy
- **Silentknife Political Operator**: Carefully removes obstacles to alliance-building

Your Archetype colors **how** you approach the Domain, not **which** Domains you can access.

## Choosing Your Archetype

### Questions to Ask Yourself

1. **How do you approach challenges?**
   - Head-on: Shieldbearer, Oathbound
   - Carefully: Silentknife, Patternweaver
   - Creatively: Songbearer, Edgerunner
   - Preservatively: Linekeeper

2. **What matters most to you?**
   - People's safety: Shieldbearer
   - Effective solutions: Silentknife
   - Stories and meaning: Songbearer
   - Honor and promises: Oathbound
   - Understanding: Patternweaver
   - Freedom: Edgerunner
   - Continuity: Linekeeper

3. **What do you risk?**
   - Your body: Shieldbearer
   - Your plans: Silentknife, Patternweaver
   - Your reputation: Songbearer, Oathbound
   - Your safety: Edgerunner
   - Your knowledge: Linekeeper

### Mix and Match

These are frameworks, not rigid categories. Your character might:
- Be primarily one Archetype with elements of another
- Shift between Archetypes in different contexts
- Struggle between competing archetypal values

Talk with your GM about nuances and blended approaches.

## Archetype and Moral Profile

Your Archetype influences your **Moral Profile** (principles you hold):

**Shieldbearer** might hold:
- "Protect the innocent" (Weight 3)
- "Never abandon my post" (Weight 2)

**Oathbound** might hold:
- "Keep my sworn word" (Weight 3)
- "Honor clan traditions" (Weight 2)

**Patternweaver** might hold:
- "Seek truth over comfort" (Weight 3)
- "Plan before acting" (Weight 1)

Acting against these principles triggers **Moral Friction** (increased Difficulty).

## Advanced: Archetype Conflicts

Some of the best character moments come from **Archetype conflicts**:

- **Shieldbearer vs Silentknife**: Protect openly vs eliminate threats quietly
- **Oathbound vs Edgerunner**: Honor rigid oaths vs adapt to necessity
- **Songbearer vs Linekeeper**: Shape new stories vs preserve old truth
- **Patternweaver vs Edgerunner**: Plan carefully vs improvise boldly

These conflicts drive character development and interesting choices.
