# Quick Reference Guide
## Essential Rules for Players

---

## Core Roll Mechanic

When taking a risky action:

> **1d20 + Stat Mod + Edge vs Difficulty**

### Difficulty Bands
- **8** - Trivial (mostly succeeds)
- **12** - Standard (real risk)
- **16** - Hard (needs skill/leverage)
- **20+** - Extreme (requires setup/tools/magic)

### Outcomes
- **Success** (beat Difficulty) - You get what you wanted
- **Partial** (exactly meet Difficulty) - You succeed with complication
- **Failure** (below Difficulty) - You don't achieve the goal

## The Eight Stats

| Stat | Abbr | Governs |
|------|------|---------|
| **Body** | BOD | Strength, endurance, physical power |
| **Reflex** | REF | Speed, agility, reaction time |
| **Charm** | CHM | Persuasion, presence, social influence |
| **Sense** | SEN | Perception, awareness, reading situations |
| **Spirit** | SPR | Willpower, conviction, mental resistance |
| **Mind** | MND | Intellect, memory, reasoning |
| **Luck** | LCK | Fortune, synchronicity, improbable success |
| **Will** | WIL | Determination, grit, pushing through |

### Stat Modifiers
- **6-7**: -2
- **8-9**: -1
- **10-11**: 0
- **12-13**: +1
- **14-15**: +2
- **16-17**: +3
- **18+**: +4

---

## Edge (Modifiers)

**Edge is a SINGLE number** combining all advantages/disadvantages:
- Usually between -3 and +3
- Maximum range: -5 to +5

### Sources of Edge
- **Archetype** (your character type)
- **Domain** (your area of expertise)
- **Tree Nodes** (specific abilities)
- **Situation** (fiction-based advantages)

**Important**: Don't stack individual bonuses - discuss the situation and agree on one Edge number.

---

## Position & Effect

### Position (How Much Risk?)
- **Controlled** - Low risk, minimal consequences
- **Risky** - Moderate risk, real consequences
- **Desperate** - High risk, severe consequences

### Effect (How Much Impact?)
- **Limited** - Partial progress, small change
- **Standard** - Normal success, expected result
- **Great** - Major success, significant change

- **Base HP** = 10 + (Body mod Ã— 2)
- Minimum: 8

### Resolve
- **Base Resolve** = 3 + (higher of Spirit or Will mod)
- Minimum: 3
- Used for: Pushing through, resisting pressure, activating abilities

### Defense
- **Base Defense** = 10 + Reflex mod + armor bonus
- Light armor: +1
- Medium armor: +2
- Heavy armor: +3
- Shield: +1

## Advancement

**You advance through story, not XP**

### Advancement Triggers (Examples)
- Form NPC relationships (mentors, contacts)
- Make meaningful choices
- Complete story milestones
- Build institutional ties
- Discover knowledge through investigation

**You unlock nodes in Domain Trees** by completing fictional triggers, not by grinding combat.

---

## Combat Quick Reference

### Battle Order System
Initiative determines when you act (see Core Rules 05)

### Standard Actions
- **Attack**: Roll vs target's Defense
- **Move**: Engage/disengage, reposition
- **Help**: Grant +1 Edge to ally's next action
- **Defend**: +2 to your Defense until your next turn
- **Special Action**: Use Tree node ability

### Harm Track
- **Level 1**: -1 to rolls, minor wound
- **Level 2**: -2 to rolls, serious wound
- **Level 3**: -3 to rolls, critical wound
- **Level 4**: Incapacitated, requires help

## Key Concepts

### When to Roll
Only roll when:
1. Outcome is uncertain
2. Failure has consequences
3. Success matters

### Fiction First
The story determines what's possible, then you translate to mechanics.

### Moral Profile
Acting against your principles triggers **Moral Friction** (increased Difficulty).

### Competencies
What you know and can do:
- **Novice** (1 point): Basic familiarity
- **Trained** (2 points): Solid competence
- **Expert** (3 points): Deep skill

## At the Table

### Player Questions to Ask
1. "What's the Difficulty?"
2. "What's my Position and Effect?"
3. "Can I get Edge from my background/archetype/situation?"
4. "What happens if I fail?"

### GM Always Answers
- What the Difficulty is
- What Position you're in
- What Effect you'll achieve on success
- What the risks are on failure

---

## Common Situations

### Group Challenges
- **Everyone rolls** if everyone's skill matters
- **Leader rolls with Edge** if one person directs
- **Best roller with disadvantage** if coordination is tricky

### Helping Others
Grant +1 Edge by assisting (describe how you help)

### Opposed Tests
Roll against target's Defense (10 + Stat Mod + Edge)

---

## Remember

1. **One roll resolves the whole action** - no extended checks
2. **Fiction first, math second** - story determines mechanics
3. **Expertise matters** - high stats + good Edge = reliable success
4. **Investigation > Combat** - this system emphasizes intelligence gathering
5. **Keep Edge simple** - one number, not a calculation puzzle
