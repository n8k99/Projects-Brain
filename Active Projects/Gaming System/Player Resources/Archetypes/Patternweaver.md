# Patternweaver
## The Analyst

---

> *"Everything has a pattern. Find the pattern, understand the system, control the outcome."*

---

## Core Philosophy

The Patternweaver's power lies in **systematic understanding**. You see connections others miss, identify underlying structures, and solve problems through **analysis rather than force**.

This isn't about raw intelligence‚Äîit's about **pattern recognition** and **methodical thinking**.

### What Drives a Patternweaver?

**Systems Thinking**: Everything is connected
- Actions have predictable consequences
- Complex problems have underlying logic
- Understanding the system lets you manipulate outcomes

**Evidence Over Assumption**: Truth comes from data
- Gather information before deciding
- Test hypotheses systematically
- Never assume when you can verify

**Preparation as Power**: Knowledge enables action
- Plan before acting
- Understand before intervening
- Informed decisions beat impulsive ones

---

## When You Get Edge

Patternweavers gain **+1 to +2 Edge** when:

### Analysis & Preparation
- **Acting on gathered intelligence** you've systematically collected
- **Exploiting patterns** you've identified in systems or behavior
- **Following plans** based on thorough analysis
- **Solving problems** through systematic understanding

### Pattern Recognition
- **Identifying connections** others missed
- **Predicting outcomes** based on observed patterns
- **Exploiting system weaknesses** you've analyzed
- **Spotting anomalies** that break expected patterns

### Methodical Approach
- **Operating with complete information**
- **Testing hypotheses** systematically
- **Executing multi-step plans** you've designed
- **Reverse-engineering systems** through observation

- Making intuitive leaps without evidence
- Improvising without analysis

**Core Betrayal (+6 Difficulty)**:

- Making decisions based on emotion over data

## Roleplaying a Patternweaver

### Key Questions for Your Character

**1. What pattern are you trying to understand?**
- Monument/Tower systems (technology)
- Political power structures (governance)
- Economic flows (resources)
- Reality itself (metaphysics)

**2. When did analysis fail you?**
- Incomplete information led you wrong
- Intuitive leap was correct, analysis wasn't
- Paralysis by analysis cost you something
- Pattern you saw wasn't there

**3. What's more important‚Äîunderstanding or action?**
- Understanding first, always (analysis paralysis risk)
- Action when understanding sufficient (pragmatic)
- Balance both (ideal but difficult)
- Sometimes act without full understanding (growth)

**4. Who taught you to think this way?**
- Mentor (systematic training)
- Trauma (chaos hurt you, order protects)
- Natural inclination (how your mind works)
- Necessity (survival required understanding)

## Patternweaver Dilemmas

### Analysis Paralysis

**The Problem**: You keep gathering data, unwilling to act without complete understanding.

But complete understanding is **impossible**. At some point, you must act.

**When do you decide you know enough?**

**Moral Friction**: Depends on principles
- "Complete information always" ‚Üí +4 to act early
- "Sufficient information" ‚Üí no friction
- "Perfect understanding" ‚Üí paralysis (character flaw)

### The Intuitive Leap

**The Problem**: Someone makes an intuitive leap that contradicts your analysis‚Äîand they're **right**.

How do you handle being **wrong**?

**Growth Moment**: Recognizing that **intuition has value**, analysis isn't everything.

### The Pattern That Isn't There

**The Problem**: You see a pattern, act on it‚Äîand it was **random chance**, not system.

**Patternweavers assume pattern**. When there isn't one, they fail.

**Learning**: Sometimes things **are** random. Not everything is system.

### The Human Element

**The Problem**: You've analyzed the system perfectly, but **people aren't acting rationally**.

Humans are **irrational**. Your perfect analysis assumes logical actors.

**What do you do when the pattern should work, but people won't follow it?**

## Patternweaver Philosophy

### Everything Is System

**Core Belief**: Chaos is just **unidentified pattern**.

Given enough data and analysis:
- Complex problems become solvable
- Seemingly random events reveal structure
- Systems can be understood and controlled

**This is both power and limitation**‚Äîassumes everything IS pattern.

### Understanding Enables Control

**Knowledge Is Power**: Understand the system ‚Üí manipulate the system.

Patternweavers seek control through **understanding**:
- Can't control what you don't understand
- Complete understanding enables precise intervention
- Ignorance is vulnerability

### The Map Is Not the Territory

**Advanced Understanding**: Your analysis is **model**, not **reality**.

High-tier Patternweavers learn:
- Models are useful but incomplete
- Abstraction loses information
- Sometimes you need to engage with messy reality, not clean models

## Advancement Advice

### Early Game (Novice-Journeyman)

**Focus**: Develop analytical skills and information gathering
- Build note-taking and observation skills
- Develop investigation techniques
- Learn to recognize patterns in specific domain

**Domain Recommendation**: Covert (Investigation) or Magic/Tech (Monument Research)

**Habit Building**: Systematic evidence collection, hypothesis testing

### Mid Game (Adept-Expert)

**Focus**: Master pattern recognition at scale
- Identify systemic patterns (not just individual clues)
- Build analytical frameworks
- Develop predictive models

**Domain Recommendation**: Multi-domain‚Äîapply pattern recognition to politics, economics, warfare

**Tree Expansion**: Intelligence Networks (data collection), Pattern Recognition (Spiritual), or Resource Intelligence (Economic)

### Late Game (Master-Apex)

**Focus**: Understand complete systems
- Recognize Orbis as designed system (Colony + AI administration)
- Predict Dragon behavior through AI pattern analysis
- See underlying code of reality

**Domain Recommendation**: Covert + Magic/Tech + Spiritual = complete systems understanding

**Legacy Goal**: Become the person who **understands how everything works**‚Äîand decides what to do with that knowledge.
