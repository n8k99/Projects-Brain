# 1
Excitement danced through the air, jumping from one resident to the next. A caravan was seen approaching up the winding glacial path to Hatfield and everyone in town knew that it would be arriving in the middle of the village by the time the bright star above was at its daily perihelion. Midday was always the best time for a caravan to pull into Hatfield. All the morning chores would be finished, the miners emerged from the copper vein for a mid-shift meal, and people could begin to exchange goods and services with the traders. Refilling vital supplies which could not be accessed in any other manner for an afternoon would be more important than gathering peat for evening fires in their homes. There might even be a feast which happens tomorrow. The size of this caravan added extra juice to this village's nerves. There were people watching the switchbacks and they could not see an end to the procession of wagons.  

Children who were at their mothers strings, danced on nerves already frazzled by the pressures of running a home within the harsh conditions atop the glacier. If their husbands had not been paid handsomely for risking their lives for the shiny orange ore they pulled from the ground, there is no way that this village would exist. All the dwarves in town lived the same life, dig deep in the mine, pull out the copper and pray to the Ember Spirits that the Flamewardens preached about that they would come back up at the end of their shift. Hatfield was not the end of the planet but it sure felt like it sometimes when the caravans traveling the Infernal Route did not come in to trade. Some mothers, some few of them, pinched their gold pieces and managed to send their children off to the few tutors in town, the hope that they would learn to read letters and write figures so that they could escape this path. 

Seldiesal Sol-astra had her healer's kit tuck under her arm as she hurried across the large square at the east edge of town. Nervously glancing at the large building which housed the Local Watch as she headed off from visiting a sick miner who was not able to report to work today. She received a little bit of coin from the town funds to do this as there was a pressure to keep as many miners as possible digging every shift. The town itself was run by other dwarves from deeper in the mine. This entire town was set up mostly for the sake of having a place to set up a market for the raw ore. So having a miner or two not show up because they had too much to drink the night before, or the cold weather made it through the thin walls of their home, or for whatever reason, was normal and the town had her go and check up on them. Today, the dwarf was actually quite ill, and barely was able to sit up when she had checked on him and administered the healing herbs. She even used a bit of a magic to help ease his suffering. Turning to the Watch Office, she walked in to collect her fees.

"Brinyar had the culp" she announced as she entered the front office.  Riminion Helcral was sitting behind his desk, scratching on a piece of parchment with a quill. The Squad Leader didn't even look up at her as she breezed into the room.

"Make sure you shut that door tightly," his voice gruff and harsh while he absently reached into a drawer of the high desk. She could hear his finger clinking coins as he absently dropped them on to the edge, just missing her outstretched hand. He made a few more marks on the page.

"I had to cast." She added with a bit more steel in her voice than she ever felt when asking for her fair rates to be honored. He looked up at her then, raised his eyebrows and grunted. His hand reached back into the drawer and this time the motion was smooth and silent before a larger, more yellow coin was dropped on her pile of silvery white coins. She caught this one before it hit the desk. "I may even have to return to see him in the morrow, it was not good."

"If it comes to that, then we'll be sending Brother Loody." the battered old soldier's voice sawed through the air, "Based off his remarks at Temple lately." And then abruptly stopped, leaving his sentence unfinished as if he were only allowed a finite number of syllables. 

"I am sure you know best." She did not feel quite like participating in this pitting her healing against the clerical services of Ralilion Loody. After all, despite being human and having deeply delusional beliefs in the power of the Ember Spirits, Brother Loody genuinely cared about the people in Hatfield. These dwarves from the deep mines who had been sent to the surface, well standing where she was, was not the place to put those thoughts into the air. 

"Oh if it isn't my favorite Elf!" Durni Trueblood had a habit of popping out of his office in the middle of transactions like this, "I do believe you were saying that it was the culp, that usually passes quite quickly, especially after you have worked your magic, hmm."

"Aye it does with the young ones, but Brinyar has been going down into the vein for decades and his lungs are not what they used to be." She countered the Overseer's argument as gracefully as she could.

"Oh but we dwarves are a hardy lot who can dig for a century or more" His freshly oiled beard hid his mouth as effectively as if he had none, while his eyes laughed merrily, silently. "I am certain that old Mr. Ironsleeves will be swinging his pick as hardily as ever tomorrow." His fingers pushed the draw on the desk closed as a full stop to his sentence. Then he smiled at her, showing his teeth. "That is if he doesn't have too much to drink tonight at the White Warrior Inn, you know as the traders will all be sorted with their rooms for the night and wanting to have a little merriment in the tavern and all that."

Her face tightened and she clasped her lips as thinly as she could. Restraining her first two or three comments that sprang to mind. "Aye, I will likely be very busy in the morning, so I should head to the house to get myself ready for a rest." Finishing putting the coins in her purse, she headed back outside.

"Make sure you close that door properly" Riminion barked at her as she was slamming it firmly.  

Outside in the crisp air, she could see that the first wagons were pulling into the western end of the village. Hatfield was so small, standing in the main square, the western end of town where the winding road entered the huddle of huts and houses was a straight shot where the top of the glacier had been leveled out decades ago. As she walked towards the gathering townspeople who were welcoming the traders to the village, she picked up the excited hubbub of the group. Everyone was running low on the necessities and the nice things had been out of everyone's pantries for weeks. Even Baoscott's Trade Post was down to just the weapons and sacks of basic products. What little fruits and produce had been in the village was gone. So it was right that everyone was excited, especially given that this caravan was so long and large. Especially given that it had come from the north where the Zhulmar Dominion borders the steppes controlled by the Coalition of Etrivalian Tribes and the Infernal Route originates a thousand miles away in Dhodh-La.

The first cart stopped right at the place where the road in reaches the Main Square. Its driver swinging down to tend to the oxen team pulling the load. Seldiesal had to navigate around it as a swarm of children and townsfolk gathered around the wagon. When she got to the rear of the boxy cabin on wheels, a door swung open and a bombastic man appeared on the porch-like platform.

"Hallo Everybody! Did you miss us?" His greeting full of amusement and merriment, as a light of joy seemed to illuminate his face from the inside out. Seldiesal noticed her daughter,  Lyra, standing back from the crowd , watching, observing, cataloging all the interactions in her mind. Seldiesal noticed that Lyra was refraining her instincts to pull out her little book and start taking notes, but her hand was hovering over the satchel where it was keep. She joined her daughter in watching the festivities of the arriving wagons. As each one came to halt, the crowd would move along and greet them. Some of the residents would return to what were quickly becoming favorite carts, such as the first one with the jolly barker who by now had produced a lute and and was playing a very excited tune. 

"Well that does it," Lyra began muttering, " Aethel is definitely going to want to leave with this group." Then she began to chuckle. Seldiesal stole a quick glance at her and could see that the emotions on her face were engaging relief, and thinking to herself that the younger twin carries too much of the world on her shoulders. 

"If they'll have his pomposity." she said, "That one there might find Aethel too much competition to have along for a months long journey." Lyra looked at her mothers face, and then both women shared a laugh at the truth of the matter.

"Oh look, it is our fearless Overseer" Lyra pointed at the Watch House with her chin, and out the front door which had been left wide open, Durni had started trotting across the square. His legs, short like the rest of him, his beard a thick shield in front of his body, immovable due to all the oil he was constantly massaging into it, even his dwarvish arms were comically double timing the patter of his feet. He had just reached the first wagon when the jolly man on the back of it swung down into the crowd. 

"We almost did not come up here!" He announced in a booming voice, Seldiesal thought that he obviously had had some bardic training the way it was projected in such a polished manner, "That gang of kobolds just past the river crossing harassed us all the way to the turn off. A smaller and less protected caravan would not risk the slow climb to your lovely little hamlet." The crowd gasped and expressed dismay at the thought of traders skipping them.  Durni had slowed to a walk and was jostling his way through the crowd. 

"Kobolds?" As he stood in front of what was so obviously the leader of the caravan and the words he had just heard were sinking into his brain, "by the river crossing? But we have patrols" His trailed off into silence. 

The silence was cast across everybody for in that instance the Mine Bell began to clang out an urgent tone. This was not the mine bell which Hatfield and its residents, its miners who dug out the copper of the higher levels of the mine would use as a signal. This was the Mine Bell which had no obvious pull rope that would tell anyone from where it was being rung. This was the Mine Bell which was put into place years ago when the troop of Deep Dwarves emerged from the lowest levels of the mines and began to thrust their control over the small village. This large iron bell was located in the very center of the Main Square and no one who had lived in Hatfield for any amount of time could recall it ever begin rung.

The toiling was insistent, penetrating all aspects of the tiny burg. The responses amongst the Hatfield people was ubiqitious; all who heard it, and that was everyone, froze in place. The only ones who seemed to be immune to its spell were the traders, Durni and those in the watch house. Shouting could be heard clearly across the square, in time to the bell.

"Up and at it you lazy good for nothings!"

"Dress Armor! Don it Now!"

"You pathetic little twits fall out and muster in the square!' 

Clearly Sgt. Helcral was in his element. This is the exact sort of moment his entire career of being a solider was about, hurrying his raw recruits into the square to respond to whatever emergency that was about to unfold. Durni Trueblood's face had lost all color. He turned and slowly began to cross the Main Square once again, ignoring the barker who was in explaining in mid-sentence how many kobolds were attacking them and for how many days and nights they suffered the ambushes.

"Hey! I'm telling you that if you don't fix this, your tiny little isolated town is going to fall off the map!" Durni had just walked away from the the negotiation he was not even aware that he had been. The bell rang and rang and rang. His steps mechanically showed what and where his actual fears were located. Riminion Helcral had managed to get two parallel lines of watch to form a gauntlet outside the Mine Entrance. The miners from Hatfield always entered the mine from a small side entrance that did not have a door, it was just the maw in the side of the mountain directly into the shaft. But there were massive wooden doors which apparently lead to another shaft altogether, and this is where the troops had formed up. Just as Overseer Trueblood stepped into the end of the formation, between the two lines of soldiers, that set of doors began to creak open.

# 2

The Mine Bell rang its final note and it was as if the spell had lifted. The townsfolk, subdued began to slowly shift from the newly arrived caravan whose carts where still creaking up the winding road to the village and clogging the main road of town. Traders would swing open their doors, expecting crowds of eager buyers and befuddled by the way everyone was silently shuffling along to the Main Square. Seldiesal spied her husband and their son, Aethel, both clutching their instruments in their hands exiting the Tavern. They had been getting ready to entertain the noon meal. She grabbed Lyra's hand and began to cut across the flow of townsfolk to the Main Square to join them. Her husband wrapped his arm across her slim elvish shoulders. His height towered above her. They had both come to Hatfield to escape the tragedy of oppressive forces. The gold dragon they had worked with, learned so much from, and felt like they were friends with, cut down twenty-five years ago. A shocking event that was unpredictable in their minds, despite Seldiesal's skills in divination. They had built a quiet and comfortable life here in the Riellis mountains, and yet this moment had the emotional tension of all that about to come crashing down yet again.

The two elves and their children seemed to be the only townsfolks who were not charmed in someway by the giant bell's toiling. All the surface dwarves were drawn to the center of the village by what only seemed to be magic or perhaps it was just part of the culture that they did not fully comprehend and for the first time in twenty-five years, they felt like outsiders. The traders from the caravan were also immune to whatever was happening, but curiously they followed along behind the milling dwarves as they pressed into the Main Square and formed a cordon behind the clustered villagers.

The massive doors to the Mine Entrance slowly, majestically creaked open, revealing a massive black hole into the side of the mountain, upon which rested the icy glacier which formed the foundation for Hatfield. Resonating from someplace deep within, the heavy clump of boots marching in time, a horn sounded and came blaring out of the mine, cutting through the trance of the crowd and a murmur spread as everyone began to nervously glance about, checking to see that their neighbors were just as alarmed and worried as they were.

Deep in the shadows of the mine shaft, a squad of heavily armed troops slowly became more visible as they marched towards the open doors. Right up to the threshold and then they halted. There were barked orders and a pair of guards escorted a third dwarf whose hands were bound together in front of him. The captive dwarf was dressed in a higher quality of leather, his cloak thrown back over his shoulders. Even though she had lived amongst dwarves of Hatfield for a quarter of a century, Seldiesal still found it difficult to determine individual features of one dwarf from another until she had been around them for significant time. However this one, while not handsome nor charismatic, she could tell was somehow different. He was still bearded, all dwarves have beards. He was still short and stocky, but this one seemed somehow wilder, more ferocious, untamed. The two guards beside him produced a key and unlocked the irons on his wrists. The troop Captain standing to the side bellowed out, "Above the foundations laid out by Forge King Meganum Drulmor , By decree of the Ruby Circle, from demand of the Ember Trial, by request of Durnek Bravone, Forgemaster of Haas Bravone, ye all witness the expulsion of this former Soulcrusher, who has willed himself to find his name upon the surface of this planet we call Orbis"

The two guards then shoved the former prisoner into the pale sunlight and stepped back into the ranks of the troop. Durni Trueblood, seemingly realized that somehow this was his cue, sang out in a cracking unsteady voice. "We, um, do accept the responsibility entrusted, um,expected in possession of this prisoner. Sargent collect him." He then stared at Riminion Helcral, who was taking a deep bellow in order to power his bellowing at his own soldiers.

The Captain of the Mine Troops, then cleared his throat, " I am afraid that you have been on the surface too long to recall that the Ember Trial is a voluntary separation from the Zhulmar Dominion. This dwarf is no more a prisoner than you."

His face turned a violent shade of red as Durni sputtered out his response, "Ah thank you for reminding me of this nuance. Of course, it is so rarely requested, and who can believe all the myths" His voice trailed off as he could see that his perspective was not being met with the same attitude that he was accustomed. "But I am glad to do our part here."

The Captain, who was on the verge of bristling during all of Durni's speech, relaxed a bit at this moment, "There is nothing you need to do, the trial is not for you." And in this moment, Korralan Bravone who had been rubbing his wrists, turned to the Captain and extended his hand. The Captain aware of this movement, slightly abashed that he forgot his duties due to the surface thoughts of an ignorant dwarf, reached with his left hand to his belt and pulled a broad and shiny axe out of it holder. He placed the handle in Korralan's outstretched hand, who weighted the weapon carefully and then slide it into place upon his back. The young dwarf turned around and face the gathered twonspeople of Hatsfield, cleared his throat before gently posing a question to the entire assembly.

"Which way to the Roadmarker of Sundering?" A gasp, a shock, a surprise, a susserration of responses as the entire collective seemed to search for the same information all at once. And then just at the same moment, there were patches of people pointing in various directions all at once. Korralan looked about, made some internal calculations and nodded. He walked through the gauntlet of watch members and when he got to Durni Trueblood who was blocking his progress, he briefly glared at the Overseer who then abashedly steppd out of the way. As Korralan moved past him, Durni then realized that he had lost whatever control he had had of the situation and then once again began to sputter out words.

"The Roadmarker? What do you want that for? It is too far, you will never make it, we have a place here for you." Not realizing that he was spinning further and further out of influence over events, Durni was metaphorically falling in line swiftly behind everything. "In fact, if you prove to be a good digger, in a couple of seasons, you could be advanced to head up a team."

The young dwarf who had stepped out of the deep mines stopped abruptly. He paused for a moment, and the entire audience of the burg of Hatsfield held their collective breathe. They sensed that what he was about to say was going to be significant, important and biting, and he did not let them down. He did not turn around and face Overseer Trueblood, which seemed to give his words even more bite as they cut through the pretentious dwarf. "I am walking away from a position I was born into that you will never reach through all your preening and posturing, do you really think that I am going to allow myself to be subjected to whatever it is you are offering me?"

Then he kept walking. The crowd slowly parted as he passed and then closed in behind him. Durni then was hit with what he thought was inspiration, "Who can show this dwarf where the kobold are ranging? Perhaps he would like to know that"
An unfazed Korralan kept walking through the silent crowd who just shuffled out of his way as they stared in awe as he passed.

A voice rang out from the back, coming from within the gaggle of traders gathered behind the townsfolk, "I'll be glad to show him." A wave of people moving out of the way opened a path between the origin of the voice and Korralan and he made the slight course correction change his bearing. The opening of the crowd, revealed a female human with a smirking expression on her face. Seldiesal was intrigued as she inspected this unlikely volunteer to venture out into the harsh biome, but her thoughts were interrupted but a pair of voices singing out in quick succession right next to her. Knowing those voices intimately as she had birthed them both on the same night minutes apart, her heart dropped as first Aethel, "I'll go with him" and then quickly Lyra, "Aye, me too." seconded him. And just like that both her children had committed themselves to an adventure which neither one knew what lay at the end of that road. 

Then they both moved into the space between the dwarf and the dark hair lady. Korralan looked at them both and continued plodding along, and they fell in behind him, both of them nervously looking about as if they were questioning the rightness of a decision which seemed to be made in haste. Seldiesal's husband sprang into motion, rushing off to their home nearby. As Korralan reached the end of the volunteer's space in the crowd, Rielan returned from their home with two packs and rushed through the gap in the crowd, pressing them into the hands of both of their children. This act seemed to calm and steady them, as if it were the permission that they needed to stick with such an impulsive action. Seldiesal's heart was simultaneously breaking and dancing for joy and she felt big heavy teardrops welling up in the corner of her eyes.

# 3
Overseer Durni Trueblood was seething and aghast. He was racking his brain as he stood there, watching the big Mine Entrance doors close behind the receding troops from deep down. He knew, he just knew that there would be some report that that Captain was going to file that would come back and bite him. How could he possibly know that dwarves from the mines would voluntarily leave the embrace of the Zhulmar Dominion. The whole ancient society which the dwarves had been building underneath this portion of Orbis for probably thousands of years. How was he supposed to know that the Ember Trial was a real thing, he had only ever heard of it in stories about heroes. The kind of stories that were read to young dwarfs when it was their bedtime. Stories that were meant to give them pride in their upbringing, teaching the nature of their culture, and to serve as warnings to not follow their leaders. All the stories he could recollect about the Ember Trial were about dwarfs who either perished out on the surface by themselves, or had some massive stroke of unbelievable luck to find something that the dwarfs needed to make their society stronger. Who had the audacity to think that their society needed to change now!

Once the massive doors had finally closed, Durni turned around and began to elbow his way through the crowd of townsfolk who had closed behind the stranger's path. This added more fuel to the roaring anger within his heart. Dwarfs who until this morning would not dare to get in his way anywhere he went in this pithy little village, insignificant folk who were just stepping stones to him, and now they were transfixed and enchanted by this supposed heroic figure who had somehow usurped the entire power dynamics of Hatfield with a grand entrance. Racking his brains as he clawed his way to where the 'prisoner' set free in his town was standing. There was that meddling cleric, Brother Ralilion Loody standing in the open circle of townsfolk and caravan traders. He appeared to be talking to the leader of the caravan. Nearby were those elf twins from Hatfield, Durni had never really liked them much, especially as he was dependent upon their mother's healing to keep miners working every day. And some human lady dressed in green leathers was standing confidently within the gather circle. Durni finished elbowing his way into their midst.

"So what is going on here?" He demanded while barely concealing his ire. Calmly, the priest from the local Flamewatchers temple turned and looked at him.

"Ah, Overseer Trueblood. Caravan Master Aloevan was just expressing his gratitude that this fine young dwarf had volunteered to take care of the gang of kobolds who have been harassing traders and travelers on the Infernal Route. Which I am sure that you and the Local Watch will greatly appreciate as it will spare you to fulfill your more important duties here within Hatfield."

"I think that would be something that the local watch and the Overseer would be keen to take care of as this village would cease to exist very shortly if smaller and less protected caravans did not risk the slow climb up the side of this glacier." the caravan master was smug and his human face did not take any measures to avoid his emotions. "Although I am going to greatly miss the very informed presence of Mistress Sol in our train, I know that she has been practically clamoring for an excuse to cut out for a couple of seasons to go snooping around in some old ruins." The woman in green leathers smiled in a manner which appeared to suggest that she'd rather laugh at this but had considered the moment inappropriate for such a display.

For some reason this really began to irk Durni, and he was about to really lay into everyone in the circle but as he was trying to decide who to let it out on first, Aethel Sol-astra had to open his pompous elf mouth.

"I have been itching to see the Roadmarker of Sundering for quite sometime, it is supposedly a magnificent monument that has stood for eons" the young bard could not restrain himself from displaying how little he thought of the dwarfs of Hatfield. The poor miners who didn't know any better might think his act was hilarious but he would was a member of the Ironhaven Clan knew that the elf lad was not pretending to be disdainful.

"And what is your reason for being here?" Durni shook his finger in the face of Lyra, Aethel's twin sister, "What do you think you will be able to do?"

"I would watch my tone with my daughter, Overseer Trueblood." the twins mother stepped into the circle, pressing a satchel into her daughter's hands. "They are both more than capable of taking care of themselves and if they have decided now is the time for them to set out on a little adventure then they have my blessings."

Fuming at the way this was turning into complete farce, Durni opened and shut his mouth a couple of times before he managed to get anything to come out. "That's just wonderful, now we will be down to only two healers in Hatfield, who will watch after the miners while she is gone?"

Brother Loody chuckled as he began speaking, "Perhaps if you were more concerned with the miners well being, you would give them a day of rest once in a while instead of flogging them into breaking rocks for your own interests both night and day. But I am just one of those healers who must keep repairing them as they break down, so what do I know?"

The strange dwarf who had emerged from the mine, seemingly having heard enough of the idle talk turned his gaze away from the Overseer and face the human woman in the green leathers, "You say you know where the Roadmarker of Sundering stands?"

"Of course I do, we passed it after travelling through the steppes. It is the first monument on the Infernal Route where the dwarfs have control. Then a couple weeks later we passed the Cliff of Sorrows before crossing the river. Which is exactly when the bandits began to ambush us."

"And if we had not be better armed, they might have done some serious damage to our chances to continue on to Akalaya" the Caravan master chimed in. "Again, I have to suggest that the local watch should have done something about this earlier."

"I had no idea." Durni began to interject into the conversation defensively.

"What I am sure Overseer Trueblood will agree is that these bandits you are reporting have been quite elusive and any of the patrols that he had sent out were avoided by them." Brother Loody's voice was attempting to calm the waters by sprinkling sugar across the surface and this only grated further on Durni's ears. 

"So you know the way?" Korralan cut through the noisy political bickering which was happening around him. When Vanya Sol nodded in the affirmative, he added. "Then let's go." And he began to head west towards the obvious exit to the town. All at once, like gravity had connected them, the circle broke apart while the three volunteers stepped into his wake, leaving Durni standing there alone. For Caravan master Aloevan and Brother Loody both moved in a different direction which seemed to be a heading to the Temple of the Comet. Only Seldiesal Sol-astra remained and even she did not face the overseer as her husband joined her. 

Instead her eyes unfocused on the scene around her, a glaze came over her face and her voice took on an eerie starkness, "You will leave a deeper mark on the Sundering Stone than you have imagined." Who she cast these words to was not obvious, however the young dwarf from the mines, paused mid-stride and then immediately began to move once again. Then she and her husband moved in yet another direction, towards the tavern. No doubt to go be quaff pints with the traders and villagers who had all gone back about their business as the day's free entertainment was obviously over and done with. 

Alone in the rapidly dispersing crowd, Durni began to sulk as he trudged back to the Watch House.

# 4
The icy road leading westward out of Hatfield was cut deep into the glacier. There were periodic piles of steaming ox dung that served as indicators that the caravan had indeed lumbered its way up the steep cutbacks. As Korralan was clearing the first cutback out of the village, Lyra piped up, "If you don't want to add more distance, there is a footpath that leads down here. Its quite a bit steeper but also faster than the road."

"How do you know of this?" Aethel was but doubtful and dumbfounded that he was not aware of this little secret, so his tone was cutting and dismissive.

"How do you think Mother and I go and gather lichen for our herbal kits from the valley and get back in time to finish dinner?" The shorter more slender twin shot back with equal temper. Korralan just stopped and waited to follow her down the trail. It was indeed quite a bit steeper than the road had been, and required a great deal of concentration from all of them as they clung to the slick glacier face, but within half an hour, they managed to reach the valley floor where the taiga took over as the dominate theme of the landscape.

Brief instances of scrub brush and rocks covered with moss and lichen were strewn about the rich brown loam underfoot. There were spots of birds in the near distance, and the scurrying of large insects as they felt the vibrations of footfalls from the four humanoids crossing the terrain.

"What do you know of the Roadmarker of Sundering?" Korralan posed his question to Vanya, when they were on a relatively flatter patch of land and it appeared that they need not have so much focus for movement. 

"It is one of the more interesting specimens of monumental architecture that I have seen in the past two years." She unfurled her response in such a way that it was obvious that she had been aching for a long time to unload this information. "It seems to be embedded in a glacier and appears to be constructed from a single piece of the largest black stone I have ever seen."

Korralan just grunted but that could have just been the effort of clearing a particularly large rock which lay in the path of the trail. Lyra had cleared the same obstacle quite easily as her elf legs were much longer. She was intrigued by something that had caught her ear, "You've been on the road for two years, where did you come from?"

"I joined the caravan as it was leaving Myranth."  Vanya dryly responded, "and have seen several different examples of monument architecture."

"Oh Myranth! I have always wanted to there and study in their famous libraries so I could learn real magic." Lyra could hardly contain herself as the secret desires came out of her. "Did you ever go to any of them?"

"Of course, but I did not get to  sit and read in any of them" the human also had very little trouble clearing the rock in the trail. Meanwhile Aethel, practically danced upon it and then gracefully twisted himself fully about as he stepped off. As his foot lighted upon the trail he blurted, "What do you mean 'monumental architecture?' I have heard those words before but never together."

Vanya smiled to herself, "There are numerous monuments dotted all across the Orbis, they all look quite similar, I would say identical but each one is ever so slightly different. They all have strange markings on them midway up as if giant stone cutters stood on each others shoulders to carve the runes into the face of the monument."

"They are all obelisk shaped." Korralan added. 

"That's right," Vanya agreed. "But they are all made of whatever is in the land nearby, so some places the monument is really hard stone and the edges are as sharp as a blade and others the stone is soft and eroded to the point of almost being round. I have not seen them but there are supposedly places where the monuments are made of metal."  They all continued on in silence for several minutes as the land got more challenging.
