# Character Creation - The Thirteen Towers

## Overview

Character creation in The Thirteen Towers is a structured process that grounds your character in the world of [[Orbis]] before play begins. You'll make choices about culture, background, archetype, and starting competencies that immediately tie you to specific locations, factions, and tensions on the map.

## Character Creation Steps

### Step 1: Concept & Culture

**Choose your character concept and cultural origin.**

Your culture determines:
- Physical and social context (what people expect of you)
- Starting language(s)
- Cultural background options
- Typical stat tendencies (optional guidance, not mandatory)

**Available Cultures:**
- [[Dail Human]] (diverse, adaptable, widespread)
- [[Celanthari]] High Elves (ancient, ritualistic, long-view planning)
- [[Dwarvish|Zhulmar Dwarves]] (mountain-hold, clan-bound, traditional)
- [[Khazadur Dwarven]] (cosmopolitan, trading, diplomatic)
- [[Etrivalian|Nomadic Peoples]] (steppe tribes, seasonal migration, oral tradition)
- Mixed Heritage (choose two cultures, navigate between worlds)
	- [[Halfling]]
	- [[Yotunn Giants]]
- [[Lothian (Dark Elfish) Spirits]]
- [[Elfish]]

**Questions to answer:**
- Where were you born? (Specific province/realm/territory)
- What do people assume about you based on appearance/accent?
- What cultural expectation do you struggle with most?

---

### Step 2: Generate Base Stats

**Roll or assign stats using one of these methods:**

#### Method A: Standard Array
Assign these values to your 8 stats as you prefer:
**14, 12, 12, 10, 10, 8, 8, 6**

#### Method B: Point Buy (27 points)
- Stats start at 8
- Raising to 9-13 costs 1 point per increase
- Raising to 14-15 costs 2 points per increase
- Maximum starting stat: 15 (before cultural/background modifiers)

#### Method C: Rolling
Roll 2d6+3 for each stat in order, then swap any two.

**The Eight Stats:**

| Stat | Abbreviation | Governs |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Body** | BOD | Raw strength, endurance, physical power |
| **Reflex** | REF | Speed, agility, reaction time, fine motor control |
| **Charm** | CHM | Persuasion, presence, social influence |
| **Sense** | SEN | Perception, awareness, reading situations |
| **Spirit** | SPR | Willpower, conviction, resistance to mental influence |
| **Mind** | MND | Intellect, memory, reasoning, analysis |
| **Luck** | LCK | Fortune, synchronicity, improbable success |
| **Will** | WIL | Determination, grit, pushing through adversity |

**Calculate Stat Modifiers:**
- Stat 6-7: -2
- Stat 8-9: -1
- Stat 10-11: 0
- Stat 12-13: +1
- Stat 14-15: +2
- Stat 16-17: +3
- Stat 18+: +4

### Step 3: Choose Background Profession

**Select a Background Profession that represents your life before adventure.**

Your background provides:
1. **Professional Trait** - A defining feature from your work
2. **Starting Competencies** - Skills and knowledge areas
3. **Hooks** - NPCs, factions, or locations you're tied to

**Example Backgrounds by Region:**

**Urban:**
- Tower-scribe Apprentice (bureaucracy, reading old texts)
- Market Guard (urban patrol, merchant connections)
- Mortuary Acolyte (death rites, spirit lore)
- Guild Apprentice (crafting, trade networks)
- Street Courier (navigation, rumor networks)

**Rural:**
- Valewarden Road-warden (rural patrol, local politics)
- Hedge Farmer (agriculture, weather lore, local spirits)
- Miller/Ferryman (river knowledge, crossroads gossip)
- Barrow-keeper (ancestor rites, death customs)
- Frontier Scout (tracking, survival, border tensions)

**Martial:**
- Garrison Soldier (formation fighting, military protocol)
- Caravan Guard (protection detail, trade routes)
- Khazadur Tunnel Guard (underground combat, clan honor)
- Tribal Warrior (raiding, honor codes, clan politics)
- Mercenary Outrider (skirmish tactics, contract work)

**Specialized:**
- Smuggler (contraband routes, bribe networks)
- Monument Tender (basic tech maintenance, ritual knowledge)
- Traveling Performer (entertainment, gathering rumors)
- Herbalist/Healer (medicine, local botany)
- Scribe/Clerk (literacy, bureaucracy, record-keeping)

**Record:**
- Background name
- One Professional Trait (mechanical benefit)
- 3-5 starting competencies
- 1-2 connection hooks (NPCs or locations)

### Step 6: Build Starting Competencies

**Competencies represent what you know and can do.**

**You have: 10 Competency Points to spend**

**Cost Structure:**
- **Novice (1 point):** Basic familiarity, can attempt with guidance
- **Trained (2 points):** Solid competence, can work independently
- **Expert (3 points):** Deep skill, can teach others, handle complex situations

**Competency Categories:**

**Knowledge:**
Languages, cultures, history, geography, lore, academic subjects

**Technical:**
Crafts, tools, instruments, vehicles, specialized equipment

**Physical:**
Combat styles, athletics, stealth, survival, performance

**Social:**
Protocols, etiquette, negotiation, intimidation, deception

**Supernatural:**
Rituals, Monument operation, anomaly navigation, spiritual practices

**Example Build - Zhulmar Tunnel Guard:**
- Combat (Trained) - 2 points
- Underground Navigation (Expert) - 3 points
- Dwarven History (Trained) - 2 points
- Clan Protocols (Novice) - 1 point
- Stone-working (Trained) - 2 points
- **Total: 10 points**

---

### Step 7: Select Starting Tree Node

**Choose your first progression node from a Tree in your Primary Domain.**

Each Domain contains multiple Trees representing different paths to power. Choose one node from any available Tree in your Primary Domain.

**Example Martial Trees:**
- Combat Prowess (personal fighting ability)
- Unit Leadership (commanding formations)
- Fortification (defensive structures)
- Logistics (supply and movement)

**Example Covert Trees:**
- Intelligence Networks (information gathering)
- Infiltration (getting where you shouldn't be)
- Assassination (eliminating targets)
- Counter-intelligence (protecting secrets)

**Your first node:**
- Must have "Novice" or "Entry" in its title
- Provides one clear permission or mechanical benefit
- May require a brief fictional justification (discuss with GM)

**Record:**
- Tree name
- Node name
- Effect/permission granted

### Step 8: Equipment & Resources

**Starting Wealth:** 50 silver coins (or equivalent in trade goods)

**Starting Equipment Package (choose one):**

**Martial Package:**
- Weapon (your choice of style)
- Armor (light or medium)
- Shield or backup weapon
- Basic field kit (bedroll, rations, waterskin)

**Covert Package:**
- Light weapon (concealable)
- Lock picks or similar tools
- Disguise kit or forgery supplies
- Urban survival kit

**Social Package:**
- Fine clothing appropriate to culture
- Writing materials
- Small gifts/trade goods
- Musical instrument or similar

**Frontier Package:**
- Practical weapon
- Survival gear (tinderbox, rope, knife)
- Navigation tools
- Animal handling gear

**Scholar Package:**
- Reference books (1-2 volumes)
- Writing/recording materials
- Tool kit for specialty
- Travel documents

**Additional Items:**
You may purchase additional items with remaining silver or justify having specific items based on your Background Profession.

---

### Step 9: Determine Starting Resources

**Hit Points (HP):**
- Base HP = 10 + (Body modifier √ó 2)
- Minimum HP: 8

**Resolve:**
- Base Resolve = 3 + (Spirit or Will modifier, whichever is higher)
- Minimum Resolve: 3
- Used for: Pushing through, resisting mental/moral pressure, activating special abilities

**Defense:**
- Base Defense = 10 + Reflex modifier + armor bonus
- Light armor: +1
- Medium armor: +2
- Heavy armor: +3
- Shield: +1 (requires hand free)

---

### Step 10: Establish Connections & Moral Profile

**Connections (choose 2-3):**
Who matters to you and why?
- Mentor/teacher from your Background
- Family member or clan connection
- Rival or enemy (complicated relationship)
- Faction or organization you're tied to
- Debt owed or obligation held

**Moral Profile:**
Define 2-3 principles your character holds:
- What won't you compromise on? (Moral Weight 3)
- What matters but you might bend? (Moral Weight 2)
- What you prefer but can violate? (Moral Weight 1)

**Examples:**
- "Protect the innocent" (Weight 3)
- "Honor clan traditions" (Weight 2)
- "Prefer honest dealing" (Weight 1)

Acting against these principles triggers **Moral Friction** - increased Difficulty on rolls.

---

### Step 11: Final Touches

**Name:** Choose a name appropriate to your culture

**Age:** Typical adventuring age ranges by culture:
- Humans: 18-45
- Dwarves: 30-120
- Elves: 25-200+

**Appearance:** Brief description focusing on:
- Distinctive features
- How your culture/background shows
- Any scars, marks, or notable traits

**Starting Location:** Where does your story begin?
- Should tie to Background Profession
- GM may specify based on campaign start

**Short-term Goal:** What are you trying to accomplish right now?

**Long-term Aspiration:** Where do you hope this path leads?

## Quick-Start Option

**For faster character creation:**

1. Choose from **pre-generated archetype templates** (when available)
2. Customize name, appearance, and one background element
3. Select one moral principle
4. Begin play, filling in details through first session

---

## Notes for GMs

- Character creation works best as **Session Zero** activity
- Help players tie backgrounds to campaign geography
- Ensure party has complementary Domains and Archetypes
- Use connection hooks to build immediate plot threads
- First Tree nodes should feel earned but accessible
