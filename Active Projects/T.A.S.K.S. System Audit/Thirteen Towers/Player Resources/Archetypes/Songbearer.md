# Songbearer
## The Story-Weaver

---

> *"The story outlives the sword. The song shapes more than the throne. I carry the narrative, and the narrative shapes reality."*

---

## Core Philosophy

The Songbearer's power lies in **narrative itself**‚Äîstories, songs, performances, and cultural memory. You understand that reality is shaped by how people **remember** and **interpret** events, not just by what actually happened.

This archetype isn't about entertainment‚Äîit's about **weaponizing meaning**.

### What Drives a Songbearer?

**Narrative as Power**: The right story can topple kingdoms
- How events are remembered matters more than the events themselves
- Public perception is a battlefield
- Symbols and stories move masses

**Cultural Stewardship**: You carry what must be remembered
- Traditions preserved through your retelling
- Truths encoded in song and story
- The past speaks through you to the present

**Performance as Truth**: The telling matters as much as the tale
- How you present shapes what people believe
- Presence and charisma make ideas real
- The messenger is part of the message

---

## When You Get Edge

Songbearers gain **+1 to +2 Edge** when:

### Shaping Narrative
- **Influencing** how events are perceived or remembered
- **Telling stories** that change minds or inspire action
- **Invoking cultural memory** or shared traditions
- **Reframing events** through different narrative lenses

### Performance & Presence
- **Public performances** before audiences
- **Inspiring groups** through song, speech, or ritual
- **Demoralizing enemies** by undermining their narrative
- **Cultural appeals** using shared stories and values

### Memory Work
- **Preserving truths** others would forget or suppress
- **Recovering lost stories** that matter to the present
- **Witnessing** important events for future telling
- **Connecting past to present** through narrative

- "Performance matters‚Äîpresentation shapes reception"
- "Every story has multiple tellings; I choose wisely"
- "Silence is complicity; I bear witness"

### Moral Friction Triggers

**Minor Bend (+2 Difficulty)**:
- Embellishing truth for better story
- Omitting inconvenient details
- Performing halfheartedly

**Significant Compromise (+4 Difficulty)**:
- Telling falsehoods as truth
- Suppressing important stories for political gain
- Allowing important events to go unrecorded

**Core Betrayal (+6 Difficulty)**:
- Deliberately erasing cultural memory
- Using narrative to destroy rather than preserve
- Betraying truth for personal gain

## Roleplaying a Songbearer

### Key Questions for Your Character

**1. What story are you telling?**
- Personal narrative (your own legend)
- Cultural preservation (your people's stories)
- Historical truth (what actually happened)
- Strategic narrative (story that achieves goals)

**2. Who is your audience?**
- Your community (cultural preservation)
- The powerful (speaking truth to power)
- Future generations (bearing witness)
- The masses (shaping public belief)

**3. What's more important‚Äîtruth or meaning?**
- Literal truth (what happened)
- Emotional truth (what it meant)
- Strategic truth (what advances your cause)
- Beautiful truth (the version people need to hear)

**4. Whose stories have been silenced?**
- The defeated (victors write history)
- The forgotten (common people, not heroes)
- The suppressed (dangerous truths)
- The lost (pre-Shattering humanity)

## Songbearer Dilemmas

### Truth vs Meaning

**The Core Tension**: Sometimes the **true** story is less meaningful than the **false** one.

Example: The heroic general was actually a coward, but his legend inspires thousands.

Do you:
- Preserve the inspiring legend (meaningful but false)
- Reveal the truth (accurate but demoralizing)
- Craft a third narrative (find truth that still inspires)

**Moral Friction**: Depends on principles‚Äî"truth above all" vs "meaning sustains communities"

### Weaponizing Narrative

**The Propagandist's Dilemma**: Your stories can start wars, topple rulers, incite violence.

Are you responsible for **how your stories are used**?

**Songbearer Philosophy**: Vary by character
- "I'm just the messenger" (abdication of responsibility)
- "I choose my stories carefully" (strategic responsibility)
- "I bear witness; consequences are others' choice" (chronicle perspective)

### The Silenced Story

**The Suppression Question**: Someone powerful wants a story **erased**.

If you tell it:
- People deserve truth
- You risk everything
- The story might cause harm

If you suppress it:
- You betray your archetype
- Truth dies with your silence
- But people might be protected

**Moral Friction**: Core Betrayal (+6) to suppress truth‚Äîbut consequences might justify it.

### Performance vs Authenticity

**The Entertainer's Trap**: Are you a **truth-teller** or a **performer**?

When performance demands change truth for better story, **what do you do**?

Songbearers must navigate:
- Embellishment (making story compelling)
- Distortion (changing facts for narrative impact)
- Fabrication (inventing "truth")

**Where's the line?**

## Songbearer Philosophy

### Narrative Shapes Reality

**Core Belief**: What people **believe** happened is more powerful than what **actually** happened.

Example:
- The battle was lost, but the story says "heroic last stand"
- Result: Morale preserved, recruits inspired, defeat becomes legend

**Songbearers understand**: Control the narrative, control the consequences.

### The Story Outlives the Sword

**Long-Term Thinking**: Empires fall, but stories endure.

Songbearers play the **long game**:
- Armies win battles today
- Stories shape civilizations for generations

**Your legacy**: Not what you did, but what **stories you left behind**.

### Witness as Responsibility

**Bearing Witness**: Someone must remember and tell what happened.

Songbearers carry the **burden of memory**:
- The forgotten deserve to be remembered
- Silence enables erasure
- Witnessing is activism

**The weight**: You carry stories of atrocity, loss, and injustice‚Äîand must retell them.

## Advancement Advice

### Early Game (Novice-Journeyman)

**Focus**: Build repertoire and audience
- Learn cultural stories and traditions
- Develop performance skills
- Build reputation as storyteller

**Domain Recommendation**: Spiritual (Lore Understanding or Belief Systems)

**Gear Priority**: Quality instrument, songbook, network of fellow performers

### Mid Game (Adept-Expert)

**Focus**: Shape belief and narrative at scale
- Your stories influence communities
- Develop strategic narrative skills
- Build audience across multiple realms

**Domain Recommendation**: Spiritual + Political (narrative legitimacy) or Spiritual + Covert (information warfare)

**Tree Expansion**: Belief Systems, Information Warfare, or Diplomacy

### Late Game (Master-Apex)

**Focus**: Reshape civilizational narrative
- Your stories define cultural identity
- Reveal or reframe fundamental truths
- Create narratives that outlive civilizations

**Domain Recommendation**: Multi-domain‚Äînarrative power supports all domains

**Legacy Goal**: Create the **origin story** the next age will tell about this one.

## Songbearer and Draconic Attention

**Low Risk**: Cultural stories, entertainment, traditional narratives

**Moderate Risk** (+2-4 Severity):
- Political narratives that destabilize realms
- Stories that question Dragon authority
- Revealing Monument "magic" as technology

**High Risk** (+6-8 Severity):
- Revealing Earth/Colony truth through performance
- Creating civilizational narratives that reject Dragon administration
- Spreading Truth through song (unstoppable once started)

**Songbearer Advantage**: Stories spread **organically**‚ÄîDragons can silence the storyteller, but not the story.

**Songbearer Danger**: Your stories might outlive you‚Äîeven if Dragons kill you, the narrative spreads.
