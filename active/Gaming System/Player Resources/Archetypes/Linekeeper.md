# Linekeeper
## The Continuity Guardian

---

> *"I am the thread connecting past to future. What came before speaks through me to what comes after. I preserve what must not be lost."*

---

## Core Philosophy

The Linekeeper's identity is defined by **preservation and continuity**. You are the guardian of tradition, the keeper of lineage, the bearer of institutional memory. You connect past to future.

This isn't about rigidity‚Äîit's about **responsibility**. The past has wisdom. The future deserves to receive it. You are the bridge.

### What Drives a Linekeeper?

**Continuity as Sacred**: The thread must not break
- You preserve what previous generations built
- Traditions encode hard-won wisdom
- Breaking continuity wastes ancestral sacrifice
- You are responsible to past AND future

**Memory as Duty**: Someone must remember
- Important truths must be preserved
- Cultural memory sustains communities
- Forgetting is a form of death
- You bear the weight of remembrance

**Tradition as Foundation**: The old ways have value
- Change requires justification
- Innovation built on preserved knowledge
- Institutions outlive individuals through continuity
- You maintain what endures

---

## When You Get Edge

Linekeepers gain **+1 to +2 Edge** when:

### Preserving & Recording
- **Documenting important events** for future memory
- **Maintaining traditions** and cultural practices
- **Recovering lost knowledge** that should be preserved
- **Acting as witness** to significant moments

### Upholding Continuity
- **Defending institutions** from disruption
- **Maintaining lineages** (family, organization, tradition)
- **Following precedent** and established procedure
- **Resisting disruptive change** without justification

### Connecting Past to Future
- **Invoking historical precedent** to guide present
- **Teaching traditions** to next generation
- **Preserving artifacts** and records
- **Honoring ancestors** and lineage

Your **Moral Profile** likely includes principles like:

### Core Principles (Weight 3)
- "I preserve what must not be forgotten"
- "Tradition carries ancestral wisdom"
- "Continuity is sacred‚Äîthe thread must not break"

### Supporting Principles (Weight 2)
- "Change requires justification"
- "I am responsible to past and future both"
- "Memory is duty, not choice"

### Personal Principles (Weight 1)
- "Institutions outlive individuals"
- "I honor those who came before"
- "The old ways have proven value"

### Moral Friction Triggers

**Minor Bend (+2 Difficulty)**:
- Bending tradition slightly for pragmatic reasons
- Allowing minor innovations without precedent
- Prioritizing present over preservation temporarily

**Significant Compromise (+4 Difficulty)**:
- Breaking with tradition significantly
- Allowing institutional disruption
- Failing to preserve important knowledge

**Core Betrayal (+6 Difficulty)**:
- Deliberately destroying cultural memory
- Abandoning lineage or tradition entirely
- Choosing erasure over preservation

## Roleplaying a Linekeeper

### Key Questions for Your Character

**1. What are you preserving?**
- Family lineage (ancestor honor)
- Cultural traditions (community memory)
- Historical knowledge (truth preservation)
- Institutional continuity (organizational memory)

**2. Why is preservation sacred to you?**
- Duty to ancestors (obligation)
- Responsibility to future (stewardship)
- Witnessed loss of important knowledge (trauma)
- Believe tradition has intrinsic value (philosophy)

**3. When is it right to let something be forgotten?**
- Never (absolutist)
- When preservation causes more harm (pragmatic)
- When tradition becomes oppressive (reformist)
- When truth serves no purpose (selective)

**4. What tradition do you carry forward?**
- Specific practice (ritual, craft, knowledge)
- Institutional role (office, position, duty)
- Family legacy (bloodline, oath, responsibility)
- Cultural memory (stories, language, customs)

## Linekeeper Dilemmas

### The Harmful Tradition

**Dilemma**: A tradition you preserve is **harmful** (oppressive, unjust, outdated).

Do you:
- Preserve it anyway (tradition √ºber alles)
- Let it die (selective preservation)
- Preserve but contextualize (historical record, not active practice)

**Moral Friction**:
- Abandoning tradition: +4 to +6
- Preserving harmful tradition: Depends on personal principles

### The Dangerous Truth

**Dilemma**: You've preserved knowledge that's **dangerous**‚Äîcould harm people, destabilize society, attract Draconic Attention.

Do you:
- Keep it secret (responsible preservation)
- Reveal it (truth over safety)
- Destroy it (safety over preservation)

**Linekeeper Tragedy**: Destroying knowledge **betrays your core identity**‚Äîbut preserving it might harm people.

### The Lost Lineage

**Dilemma**: A tradition/lineage is **dying**. You're the last keeper.

If you don't pass it on, it **dies with you**.

But there are **no worthy successors** (everyone is incompetent, disrespectful, or disinterested).

**What do you do?**

**Options**:
- Train unworthy successor (tradition survives, degraded)
- Let it die with you (maintain purity, lose continuity)
- Record it completely (preserve knowledge without living tradition)

### The Innovation vs Tradition Conflict

**Dilemma**: Someone proposes **innovation** that **improves** on tradition.

Traditional method: Slower but proven
Innovative method: Faster but untested

**Do you resist improvement to preserve tradition?**

**Linekeeper Growth**: Learning that **preservation doesn't mean stagnation**‚Äîtraditions can evolve.

## Linekeeper Philosophy

### The Thread Must Not Break

**Core Belief**: Continuity from past to future is **sacred**.

When continuity breaks:
- Institutional knowledge is lost
- Communities lose identity
- Ancestral wisdom is wasted

Linekeepers maintain the **thread**‚Äîthey are the connection.

### Memory as Responsibility

**Burden of Remembrance**: You **must** remember. It's not optional.

What you forget, **the world forgets**.

This is:
- Heavy (carrying weight of memory)
- Sacred (you're trusted with this)
- Essential (someone must do it)

### Tradition as Encoded Wisdom

**Why Preserve Tradition?**: It contains **lessons learned through suffering**.

Traditions exist because:
- Ancestors tried alternatives that failed
- Practices that work get passed down
- Cultural solutions encode local wisdom

**Dismissing tradition = dismissing ancestral wisdom.**

But **evaluating** tradition ‚â† dismissing it.

- Both value continuity and tradition

- Songbearer makes memory meaningful through story

- Linekeeper provides historical data for Patternweaver's analysis

- Low conflict unless Silentknife disrupts what Linekeeper preserves

- Both serve something larger than themselves
- Shieldbearer protects people; Linekeeper protects knowledge
- Mutual respect for duty

**+ Edgerunner**: Philosophical opposition
- Edgerunner pushes boundaries; Linekeeper maintains them
- Edgerunner embraces change; Linekeeper resists it
- Fundamental conflict over value of tradition vs innovation

## Advancement Advice

### Early Game (Novice-Journeyman)

**Focus**: Establish yourself as tradition-keeper
- Learn what needs preserving (lore, craft, tradition)
- Build reputation for accurate preservation
- Begin documenting or maintaining important knowledge

**Domain Recommendation**: Spiritual (Lore Understanding) or Magic/Tech (Chronicle Decipherment)

**Habit Building**: Systematic documentation, careful preservation

### Mid Game (Adept-Expert)

**Focus**: Become recognized authority on preservation
- Recover lost knowledge
- Become institutional memory keeper
- Build archives or preservation systems

**Domain Recommendation**: Spiritual + Magic/Tech (if preserving Chronicles) or Spiritual + Political (if maintaining institutions)

**Reputation**: Continental recognition as preservation authority

### Late Game (Master-Apex)

**Focus**: Become living embodiment of continuity
- You ARE the institutional/cultural memory
- What you preserve survives; what you forget dies
- Build systems that preserve beyond your death

**Domain Recommendation**: Master-tier Spiritual (cultural memory), possibly Magic/Tech (complete Chronicles)

**Legacy Goal**: Create **preservation systems** that outlive you‚Äîarchives, institutions, trained successors

## Linekeeper and Draconic Attention

**Generally Low Risk**: Preserving tradition doesn't threaten Dragons

**Moderate Risk** (+2-4):
- Preserving political traditions that challenge Dragon authority
- Maintaining institutional memory that questions Dragon legitimacy

**High Risk** (+6-8):
- Preserving complete Chronicle records (Earth-Colony-Shattering truth)
- Teaching civilization its true origin
- Maintaining records Dragons want erased

**Linekeeper Dilemma**: If Dragons order you to **destroy** records, **do you?**

Destroying knowledge is **core betrayal** (+6 Moral Friction)‚Äîbut defying Dragons is **suicidal**.

Many Linekeepers choose **martyrdom** over erasure.
